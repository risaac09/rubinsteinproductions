# Rubinstein Productions — Outreach System v2
## "The Digital Liver" Framework

*Fresh start. March 2026. The old cycle completed. This is what remains.*

---

## Core Principle

The outreach system mirrors the methodology: metabolize before you speak. Every outreach touch should demonstrate the work, not describe it. No cold pitching. No performance. Simple, true statements that create pull.

---

## The 3-Touch Metabolize Sequence

Each potential relationship moves through three touches — matching the liver's function: **Filter → Process → Nourish.**

### Touch 1: Filter (Observe + Select)
**Purpose:** Identify people whose work shows signs of narrative dysregulation — the gap between what they do and how they talk about it.

**Where to look:**
- LinkedIn posts that hedge, over-explain, or hide the real point
- Nonprofit websites with jargon-heavy "About" pages
- Conference speakers whose bios don't match their actual energy
- Grant-funded orgs doing extraordinary work with terrible messaging

**Action:** Engage authentically with their content. Comment with something specific and true. No pitch. No link. Just proof you see their work clearly.

**Template (comment, not DM):**
> "This is the part most people won't say out loud: [specific thing from their post]. The fact that you did matters."

---

### Touch 2: Process (Offer a Mirror)
**Purpose:** After genuine engagement (1-2 weeks), offer something that demonstrates the facilitation work without selling it.

**Channels:** DM or email, depending on where the relationship started.

**Template — The Mirror Email:**

Subject: What I noticed about [their work / their org]

---

[First name],

I've been following [specific thing — their project, their posts, their org's work] for a few weeks. One thing keeps standing out to me:

[One true observation about the gap between their impact and how they communicate it. Be specific. Be generous. Name what's real.]

I work with mission-driven people on exactly this — closing the gap between what you do and how you're heard. It starts with a 90-minute facilitated conversation.

No pitch. Just wanted to name what I'm seeing. If that resonates, I'm happy to talk.

Isaac Rubinstein
Rubinstein Productions
[website link]

---

**Key constraints:**
- Never send this to more than 3 people per week
- Each email must contain a specific, true observation (no templates that could apply to anyone)
- If you can't name something specific, you haven't filtered well enough — go back to Touch 1

---

### Touch 3: Nourish (Invite to Experience)
**Purpose:** For people who responded to Touch 2, offer a direct experience of the work.

**Options (choose based on context):**
- **The full offering** — Facilitated conversations + a camera and lens shipped to them + they film themselves + 3-min brand video + all raw footage returned. "It starts with a conversation. Then a camera shows up at your door."
- **Introductory conversation** — A single facilitated session. No camera, no deliverable. Just the experience of being listened to. See if the relationship has legs.
- **Content collaboration** — "I'd love to feature your work in a piece I'm writing about [their domain]." Gives before asking.

**Template — The Invitation:**

Subject: Re: [previous thread]

---

[First name],

Glad that landed. Here's what I'd suggest as a next step:

[One specific option — not three. Choose the right one for this person.]

[2-3 sentences about what they'd get / what happens / what it costs. Direct. No hedging.]

If the timing isn't right, no pressure. The observation stands either way.

Isaac

| Activity | Frequency | Volume |
|---|---|---|

## What We Let Go Of (from the old system)

- ~~Airtable pipeline tracking~~ → Replaced with a simple spreadsheet. The tool was bigger than the practice.
- ~~Cold outreach templates~~ → Every touch is specific or it doesn't get sent.
- ~~Volume metrics~~ → Replaced with quality constraint: 3 Mirror emails/week max.
- ~~CRM-style follow-up sequences~~ → If someone doesn't respond, they don't respond. No drip campaigns.
- ~~"Outreach manager" skill automation~~ → The outreach IS the methodology demonstration. It can't be automated.

---

## What We Keep

- **Gmail drafts** — still useful for composing and reviewing before sending
- **Simple tracking** — a single-sheet log: Name, Org, Date, Touch #, Response (Y/N), Notes
- **The constraint** — never send what you wouldn't say in person
- **The test** — does this email demonstrate the Digital Liver? Does it filter, process, and nourish? Or is it performing outreach?

---

## Measurement (Bilingual)

**Royal Register (what counts):**
- Conversations started per month
- Sessions booked per quarter
- Revenue from outreach-originated relationships

**Nomadic Register (what matters):**
- Did the Mirror email contain a true observation?
- Did the person feel seen, not sold to?
- Is the outreach practice itself nourishing or depleting?

---

## The Digital Liver Test

Before any outreach action, ask:

1. **Is this filtered?** Have I actually observed this person's work, or am I pattern-matching?
2. **Is this processed?** Can I name something specific and true about the gap between their impact and their communication?
3. **Is this nourishing?** Would receiving this message feel like a gift, regardless of whether they hire me?

If any answer is no — stop. Go back to observation. The liver doesn't rush.
