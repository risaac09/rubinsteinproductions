bio · long

Isaac Rubinstein is a facilitator and filmmaker based in Providence, Rhode Island. He runs Rubinstein Productions, a facilitation and film practice for mission-driven leaders whose capacity for honest, clear expression has been alienated by the institutional environments they operate inside.

Isaac entered medicine at the bedside — pediatric psychiatric direct care — and returned at the systems level with an MPH and a series of evaluation engagements embedded in collective-impact and change-management contexts. He has stood up shared measurement systems for a 60+ partner Health Equity Zone collaborative, led change-management readiness for a healthcare ERP rollout at a major pediatric hospital, and run participatory program evaluation for community-based organizations.

The methodology he teaches and uses is called Say Why. It is a four-phase arc — Container, Excavation, Performance, Translation — grounded in polyvagal theory, participatory evaluation, and community-based system dynamics. The offering is anti-extractive by design: a camera ships, the client films themselves, all footage and data are returned. He is currently publishing an 8-essay theoretical arc on knowledge metabolism, somatic practice, and the seam where institutions meet the body.
