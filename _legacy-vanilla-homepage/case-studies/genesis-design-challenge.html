What it was.

An S3 Genesis Design Challenge — a 10-week embedded engagement with an 8-person cohort working on a regenerative funding model in collaboration with Open Circle Foundation. The work combined facilitated wayfinding conversations, on-camera narrative capture, and a longitudinal pulse-survey impact evaluation that produced data across the full 10-week arc.

Why it matters.

Genesis was the first end-to-end run of the Say Why methodology — Container, Excavation, Performance, Translation — across a real cohort, with the camera shipping to participants and all footage returned. The compilation film and the longitudinal pulse-survey instrument that came out of this engagement are the proof-of-process artifacts that subsequent paid engagements draw on.

A 3-minute compilation film is published on YouTube. An impact evaluation report with longitudinal pulse-survey data is available on request.

What it produced.

  • Wayfinding interviews with each cohort participant
  • A 3-minute compilation film capturing the cohort's collective trajectory
  • A longitudinal pulse-survey instrument administered across the 10-week arc
  • An impact evaluation report drawing on both the qualitative interview corpus and the pulse-survey time series

What it taught the practice.

  • The Sacred Pause — the 5-day minimum between filming and editing — emerged as a non-negotiable rhythm during this engagement.
  • Pulse-survey instrumentation across a 10-week arc captures shifts that a single pre/post comparison would miss.
  • Embedded evaluation works at this depth when the evaluator is a recognized cohort participant rather than an outside observer.
