about · isaac rubinstein

Facilitator
and filmmaker.

I work with mission-driven leaders to recover the inside version of their truth and build the capacity to express it on camera. The methodology is called Say Why.

I entered medicine at the bedside and returned at the systems level. The work I do across institutions is one continuous loop — practitioner ↔ patient · the body preconditioning the mind · the inside version made public.

The diagnosis.

Most organizations are stuck between knowing what's true and saying it. The messaging sounds generic because the articulation happens downstream of lived experience, not inside it.

The gap isn't a communications problem. It's a safety problem — the institutional environment has made honesty feel expensive. Jargon, hedging, over-explaining, and silence are rational responses to systems that make honesty cost something.

The condition is structural, not personal. The work is relational, not heroic.

The practice.

Rubinstein Productions is a facilitation and film practice. The methodology is called Say Why — a four-phase arc grounded in polyvagal theory, participatory evaluation, and community-based system dynamics.

The offering is physical and anti-extractive. A camera ships. The client films themselves. Everything is returned. Nothing is kept.

  • Container. Relational safety and institutional permission, built simultaneously. A logic model the organization can sign off on.
  • Excavation. Surface the inside version. Map the feedback loops that keep honest expression contained.
  • Performance. The camera enters. The client holds it. I facilitate remotely. Zero-measurement zone — emergence happens because it isn't being measured.
  • Translation. The living thing becomes an artifact that can travel. A 3-minute brand video. All footage and data returned. Bilingual Dashboard for engagements at depth.

What it isn't.

  • Not coaching. No ongoing extraction.
  • Not messaging strategy. I don't write FOR you — I help you recover what's already there.
  • Not corporate mindfulness. McMindfulness is the problem, not the cure.
  • Not video production. The camera is a witness, not a polishing tool.
  • Not a framework you install. It's a container you enter.

Background.

MPH. Pediatric psychiatric direct-care background. Years of evaluation work — utilization-focused, participatory, mixed-methods — embedded inside change-management and collective-impact contexts.

I've sat with kids in crisis on a unit. I've stood up shared measurement systems for collective-impact collaboratives reporting to multiple funders. I've led change-readiness for a healthcare ERP rollout. The thread across all of it is the same: nervous-system safety produces narrative coherence.
