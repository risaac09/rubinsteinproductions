Case study · 04

Workday Foundation — workforce program portfolio review.

A corporate foundation funding workforce development across multiple grantees needed evidence its portfolio was producing change at the population level — not just outputs at the program level.

Role
Evaluation Consultant
Period
2023 — 2024
Funder
Corporate foundation
Domain
Workforce development

01 Context

Workday Foundation funded a portfolio of workforce development programs across multiple grantees, sites, and target populations. Each grantee reported its own indicators on its own cadence. The foundation could see activity but not portfolio-level change.

02 What the report had to change.

  • Foundation board — is the portfolio producing measurable workforce outcomes?
  • Program officers — which grantees need additional support, and which strategies are scaling?
  • Grantees — how does my program contribute to portfolio outcomes I can't see alone?

03 Approach

  • Designed a common outcomes framework across the grantee portfolio without requiring grantees to abandon their existing systems.
  • Built a portfolio-level dashboard that aggregated grantee data into population-level indicators.
  • Wrote learning briefs that translated portfolio findings into grantee-level guidance.

Outcome

Portfolio review shifted from "what did each grantee do?" to "what is the portfolio producing, and where do we invest next?" — with the same data the grantees were already collecting.

05 What this work teaches.

Foundations rarely have a measurement problem. They have an aggregation problem. The infrastructure that makes grantee-level data legible at portfolio level is usually the unbuilt piece — and it's the piece that lets the foundation answer the questions it was actually formed to answer.
