Case study · 01

Central Providence Health Equity Zone — shared measurement.

A 60+ partner community coalition needed evaluation infrastructure that could carry federal reporting and inform local decisions in the same document. Two reporting cycles, both accepted on first submission.

Role
Lead Evaluator
Period
2023 — present
Funder
Federal & local
Scope
60+ coalition partners

01 Context

The Central Providence coalition had grown to 60+ partner organizations working across housing stability, workforce development, and resident engagement. Each partner had its own intake, its own reporting, its own definition of "served." The federal funder wanted a unified annual report; the coalition director needed quarterly insights to steer the work; resident leaders needed something they could read at a community meeting.

Three audiences. One dataset.

02 What the report had to change.

Before any data collection, we named the decisions each audience faced and what the report had to put in front of them:

  • Funder — does the program meet annual benchmarks for continued investment?
  • Coalition director — which partners need additional support, and which subpopulations are being missed?
  • Resident leaders — is the work doing what we said it would do, in terms our community can verify?

Those three questions became the spine of the evaluation plan. Every indicator and every data source had to earn its place against at least one of them.

03 Approach

I designed a shared measurement system that any partner organization could use without buying new software or hiring a data analyst:

  • A common intake schema that mapped to each partner's existing tools.
  • Quarterly cross-site learning sessions where partners interpreted their own data alongside aggregate findings.
  • A resident-voice survey (n=400+) paired with 24 stakeholder interviews, coded in NVivo and integrated into the quarterly narrative.
  • A reporting cadence that produced one source document, formatted three ways for three audiences.

04 Outcomes

Outcome

"For the first time we could read one report and answer the funder's question, our director's question, and our community's question — without writing three different reports."

The coalition was retained for a follow-on engagement. The shared measurement framework has been adopted as the baseline for two newer coalition cohorts.

05 What this work teaches.

The hardest part wasn't the methodology. It was getting all three audiences to agree, in advance, on what the report had to put in front of them. Once that was named, the design followed. Most evaluations skip that conversation and try to compensate later with longer reports — which is why most reports go unread.
