RIPCA FFY2026 traffic safety — indicator design.

A statewide traffic safety program needed an evaluation plan that could survive federal review and steer mid-year programmatic decisions. We wrote one document for both readers.

Role
Evaluation Lead
Period
FFY 2026
Funder
NHTSA · Federal
Domain
Traffic safety

01 Context

The Rhode Island Police Chiefs' Association needed an evaluation framework for its FFY2026 federally-funded traffic safety initiative. The work spanned impaired driving, occupant protection, and speed enforcement across multiple agencies — each with its own data system and reporting cadence.

02 What the report had to change.

  • Federal program officer — does this program meet NHTSA performance benchmarks?
  • RIPCA leadership — which enforcement strategies are producing measurable change, and where should next year's resources go?
  • Participating agencies — what does my department's contribution look like in context?

03 Approach

  • Designed a tiered indicator set tied to NHTSA Annual Report performance measures.
  • Built a logic model that connected enforcement activity to behavioral outcomes to crash trends.
  • Produced an evaluation plan agencies could read alongside their data — not a separate appendix.

Outcome

The evaluation plan was adopted as the program's working document — used for federal reporting and quarterly steering decisions without a separate translation.

05 What this work teaches.

Federal performance measures are usually treated as a compliance overlay — written after the program has been designed. Treated as the spine of the evaluation plan instead, they accelerate local decision-making rather than getting in its way.
