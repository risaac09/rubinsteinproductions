Case study · 03

Seattle Children's — equity in pediatric care delivery.

A pediatric health system needed to translate equity commitments into measurable practice change. We built the indicator framework that connected stated values to clinical workflow.

Role
Evaluation Consultant
Period
2022 — 2024
Setting
Pediatric health system
Domain
Health equity

01 Context

Seattle Children's had committed publicly to advancing equity in pediatric care delivery. The next step was operational: defining what change looked like at the encounter level, and how the system would know when it was happening.

02 What the report had to change.

  • Executive leadership — is the equity commitment producing measurable change in care delivery, or only in language?
  • Clinical service lines — where in our workflow are the gaps, and what's the next intervention?
  • Family advisors — does the data reflect what families are actually experiencing?

03 Approach

  • Built an indicator framework spanning access, experience, and outcomes — stratified by language, race, and insurance.
  • Paired quantitative measures with structured family-advisor review at each reporting cycle.
  • Designed dashboards keyed to the decisions clinical leaders were already making — not separate equity dashboards that sit beside the work.

04 Outcomes

12+
Service lines on shared equity indicator set
3
Stratification dimensions across all measures
1
Family-advisor review built into reporting cycle
Outcome

Equity moved from an annual narrative section to a recurring agenda item in operational review — with the same indicators, in the same dashboards, that drive everything else.

05 What this work teaches.

Equity dashboards that live separately from operational dashboards stop getting used. The discipline is harder: stratify the measures the system already cares about, and make the gaps visible inside the conversations leaders are already having.
